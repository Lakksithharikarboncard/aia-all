AI Accountant Billing & Access PRD: V0

AI Accountant · Billing and Access Control Module · Version 0

Status: V0 Scope Updated: 25 May 2026

Problem Statement

AIA automates financial document processing for Indian SMBs: Accounts Payable, Accounts Receivable, Bank Statements, GST Reconciliation, Reporting, and Tally / Zoho integration.

Every customer today goes through a sales conversation before they can use the product. That's intentional. What's not intentional is everything that happens after the deal closes.

There is no billing module.

  • The team has no single place to create a customer record and send a payment link.
  • Access is enabled manually, after someone confirms payment over chat.
  • There is no record of who created a customer, what they were charged, or when payment arrived.
  • Renewals are followed up by hand.

The product works. The commercial layer around it doesn't exist yet. V0 establishes the minimum viable foundation: create a customer, set a price, send a payment link, and let Dodo Payments handle the rest.

Customer Lifecycle

The complete journey a customer goes through, from profile creation to cancellation. Status is set automatically by Dodo Payments via webhooks after the checkout link has been used. Before that, status advances only when CS takes an explicit action.

High-Level Approach & Goals

This is not a self-serve billing system. AIA is sold through demos and that stays the same.

What we're building in V0 is a minimal control layer, the thinnest possible thing that takes a closed deal to a paying customer without manual coordination. No trials. No grace periods. No module-level access control. Those come in a later version. V0 is about getting the foundation right: one place to create a customer, one price to set, one link to send, and one button inside the product for the customer to manage their subscription.

The split is simple:

  • Dodo Payments handles money: checkout, payments, subscriptions, invoices, renewals
  • AIA handles status: which customer is active, what they're paying, when it renews

What this is not (V0)

No trial periods. No grace period. A failed payment moves the account to payment_failed immediately. No per-module access control, all modules are always on. No standalone customer portal. No self-serve signup. No public pricing page. No automated pricing recommendation; CS picks the price. No token tracking. No usage-based billing. Those come in V1 and beyond.

Solution & Key Features

One module. Two surfaces: an admin portal for the CS and Growth team, and a lightweight billing touchpoint inside the AIA product for the customer. The customer-facing surface in V0 is minimal. It is not a standalone portal. It is a single "Billing Plan" button inside the AIA product that opens Dodo's hosted customer portal for subscription and invoice management. That button is only reachable after the customer has successfully signed up and paid.

CS owns the full activation lifecycle, from creating the record to sending the link. Dodo Payments handles everything after the customer pays. The AIA product handles the post-payment experience.

What Happens When

What happens when a customer pays?

Dodo fires a subscription.active webhook. AIA sets the account to active, records activatedAt, and sets the renewal date from Dodo's next_billing_date. The customer is redirected to https://app.aiaccountant.com/organisations/create-organisation after checkout completes to begin setting up their organisation.

How does the Dodo checkout page get pre-filled with the customer's details?

When the AIA sign-up page calls GET /api/customers/{customerId}/checkout, it passes the name and email the customer entered in the sign-up form as query parameters. The endpoint updates the Dodo customer record with those details before creating the checkout session. This way the checkout page arrives with the customer's actual name and email already filled in, rather than whatever CS had on file when the payment link was originally generated.

What happens when a payment fails?

Dodo fires subscription.on_hold or subscription.failed. AIA sets the account to payment_failed immediately. There is no grace period in V0. CS sees the account flagged in the Overview attention strip and follows up with the customer directly. When the customer pays, Dodo fires subscription.active and status returns to active automatically.

What happens when a subscription is cancelled?

Dodo fires subscription.cancelled. AIA sets the account to inactive. The customer can no longer access the product. If they want to reactivate, CS generates a new payment link and the cycle starts again.

The customer just paid but the admin portal still shows Payment Pending. What now?

CS opens Customer Detail, Payments tab, and clicks Resync from Dodo. This triggers a live pull from Dodo. If payment has landed, status updates to active immediately. No customer action is needed.

Can an inactive customer come back?

Yes. CS opens the customer record, clicks Generate Payment Link, and shares the new sign-up URL. When the customer pays on the new checkout, Dodo fires subscription.active and the account moves to active again.

What happens when a phone number is invalid during checkout?

The system strips spaces and non-E.164 characters before sending to Dodo. If the result is not a valid E.164 number (must start with + followed by 10 to 15 digits), the phone field is silently omitted. Dodo does not require phone. Checkout proceeds.

What happens if the customer already has a Dodo product or customer ID?

Both are reused. Only a fresh checkout session is created. This is how Regenerate Payment Link works: it reuses the existing Dodo product and customer and only creates a new session, so the customer is not double-billed.

How does AIA find the right customer when a webhook arrives?

The checkout session is created with metadata.customer_account_id set to the AIA customer ID. When a webhook arrives, the handler first looks up the customer by this metadata field. If that fails, it falls back to matching by dodoCustomerId. If neither matches, the event is logged but no state change is made.

What happens when the same webhook arrives twice?

The webhook handler checks the webhook-id header against the last 1,000 processed IDs stored in .data/webhook-events.json. If the ID is found, the event is acknowledged (200 OK) but not processed again. This prevents double-status-changes from retry storms.

What happens when billing.json is empty (fresh deploy)?

When CS generates a payment link, the checkout API route receives the customer data inline in the request body and writes it to billing.json before proceeding. This ensures webhook handlers can find the customer even on a fresh deployment where billing.json has not yet been seeded from localStorage.

How does Growth know the first payment landed?

Dodo's webhook updates the account to active automatically. The customer moves from Payment Pending to Active in the customer list and disappears from the attention strip. No manual check needed. The audit log also records the webhook event.

What exactly is logged in the audit trail?

Every change to a customer account is recorded. Each entry shows who made the change (actor), what changed (old value and new value), why (reason), and when (timestamp). Dodo webhook events are also logged with the event type as the action.

Do Growth and CS have different permissions?

Both have full access to the admin portal. The difference is who does what, not what they can see or do. Any @karboncard.com account can sign in and access all features.

What is the OTP login flow in production vs development?

In development, the OTP is returned in the redirect URL and displayed as a yellow badge on the verify page. In production, an email service (e.g. Resend) must be wired into the /api/auth/send-otp route to deliver the code. The session TTL is 8 hours. OTPs expire in 10 minutes.

How does the customer access the Dodo billing portal from inside AIA?

The customer clicks "Billing Plan" inside the AIA product. AIA calls POST /api/dodo/customer-portal with the customer's account ID. The route looks up the Dodo customer ID from the billing record, creates a short-lived Dodo portal session, and returns the URL. The customer is redirected there with no separate Dodo login needed. The session is generated server-side using their Dodo customer ID. This button is only surfaced inside the AIA product after the customer has paid and their account is active.

What happens when the customer clicks "Billing Plan" and their account is not yet active?

The "Billing Plan" button is controlled by the AIA product and is only shown when the customer is active. The /api/dodo/customer-portal route requires a linked Dodo customer ID. If called before a checkout link has been generated, it returns a 400 error. In practice this path cannot be reached because the button is not shown until after first payment.

What happens when a customer is deleted?

The customer record is permanently removed from localStorage and billing.json. This cannot be undone. The confirmation modal requires typing the exact company name. Any future Dodo webhooks for that customer will not find a matching record and will be silently ignored.

What is a Plan Preset and how is it different from the full plan catalogue?

In V0, a plan preset is a named pricing template with a name, price, billing frequency, and optional description. It exists to save CS from retyping the same price and frequency for common packages. It does not create a Dodo product at save time, and it does not control module access. All modules are always on in V0. The Dodo product is created only when a checkout link is generated for a specific customer.
