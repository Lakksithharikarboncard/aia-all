AI Accountant Billing & Access PRD: V0

AI Accountant · Billing and Access Control Module · Version 0

Status: V0 Scope Updated: 25 June 2026

Problem Statement

AI Accountant automates financial document processing for Indian SMBs. Every customer goes through a sales conversation before they can use the product — that stays. What doesn't work is everything after the deal closes.

  • BD has no single place to calculate a price, assign a plan, and send a payment link.
  • Pricing is done manually per customer (bills volume, bank connections) with no tooling.
  • Plan assignment and payment collection are coordinated over chat.
  • There is no record of who was charged what or when payment arrived.
  • Renewals and payment retries are followed up by hand.

Scope

A self-serve billing system is not the goal. AIA is sold through demos and will stay that way. V0 is a thin control layer for the BD team: calculate pricing, provision a customer in Zoho, generate a signup URL, and confirm post-payment activation — all without switching tools.

In scope:

  • Pricing calculator (Base / Premium × Quarterly / Annual) with usage sliders
  • One-click provisioning: Zoho customer + subscription + invoice payment hosted page
  • Signup URL that takes the customer to a branded page, then to Zoho checkout
  • Post-payment organisation creation via Zoho redirect callback
  • Share links via mailto (email client) and wa.me (WhatsApp)
  • Optional free trial: checkbox to enable, number input for trial duration in days
  • Admin dashboard with calculator launch, system status, quick-start guide

Out of scope (V0):

  • No self-serve signup or public pricing page
  • No grace periods
  • No per-module access control — all modules always on
  • No automated email API — uses mailto (BD reviews and sends)
  • No admin login/auth — portal is network-restricted
  • No webhook handling — post-payment confirmation uses Zoho's hosted page redirect
  • No monthly billing — Quarterly and Annual only
  • No automated pricing recommendation — BD computes via calculator

Feature Breakdown

1. Pricing Calculator

How it works. The customer picks a plan (Base / Premium) and a billing cycle (Quarterly / Annual), then enters their bills per month and number of banks (statements pulled per month). The calculator returns a final price. All costs below already include margin — just plug them in.

Per-account costs

CostBase — QuarterlyPremium — QuarterlyBase — AnnualPremium — Annual
Infra (per month)₹133.33₹133.33₹106.67₹106.67
Per bill₹7.33₹8.27₹5.87₹6.61
Per bank statement (per month)₹46.67₹146.67₹37.33₹117.33

Final price

Use the column matching the chosen plan + cycle, then:

months      = 3 (Quarterly) or 12 (Annual)
final_price = months × [ infra + (per_bill × bills_per_month) + (per_bank × banks) ]
Round the final price to the nearest rupee.
(Keep full precision while calculating; round only the final total.)

Example

Base, Quarterly, 50 bills/month, 2 banks:
3 × [133.33 + (7.33 × 50) + (46.67 × 2)] = ₹1780

Edge Cases & Decisions

Why three API calls instead of hostedpage/newsubscription?

POST /hostedpages/newsubscription defers subscription creation until payment. That means the plan is not visible in Zoho until the lead pays — which could be days later. BD needs immediate confirmation that the plan was assigned. The three-call approach creates the subscription proactively.

What happens when a hosted page expires?

Zoho invoice payment pages expire after 60 minutes. If the lead opens the link after expiry, the "Continue to Payment" button redirects to an expired Zoho page. The lead contacts BD, who re-runs the calculator to generate a fresh link.

What happens when the signup URL has missing/invalid params?

The signup page shows "Invalid or expired link. Please contact the sender for a new link." The Continue button is replaced. BD must generate a fresh link.

What happens when Zoho is unreachable?

The API has a 20-second timeout. If Zoho is down, the BD sees an inline error message. No partial state is created. Retry once Zoho is available.

Can a cancelled customer come back?

Yes. BD generates a new signup link. The existing Zoho customer is reused; a fresh subscription and hosted page are created.

Will the price override persist beyond the first billing cycle?

Unknown — needs sandbox testing. Zoho's plan.price override may reset to the plan default at renewal. Mitigation: PUT /subscriptions/{id} before each renewal to re-set the price.

Why no automated email (Resend)?

mailto gives BD control to personalise each message before sending. An automated email API can be added later if volume justifies it. WhatsApp via wa.me is always available.

How does the free trial work?

When BD checks "Enable free trial" and enters a number of days, the subscription is created in Zoho with plan.trial_days set. During the trial period the customer has full product access with no payment required. When the trial expires, Zoho generates the first invoice and the normal billing cycle begins. If the customer does not pay after the trial, the subscription moves to Past Due as per the standard lifecycle.

Why no admin login?

The portal is network-restricted (internal URL, not publicly advertised). Auth (OTP, domain-restricted) is deferred. The team accesses it directly.
