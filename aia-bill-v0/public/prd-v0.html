AI Accountant Billing & Access PRD: V0

AI Accountant · Billing and Access Control Module · Version 0

Status: V0 Scope Updated: 12 June 2026

Problem Statement

AIA automates financial document processing for Indian SMBs: Accounts Payable, Accounts Receivable, Bank Statements, GST Reconciliation, Reporting, and Tally / Zoho Books integration.

Every customer today goes through a sales conversation before they can use the product. That's intentional. What's not intentional is everything that happens after the deal closes.

There is no billing module.

  • The team has no single place to provision a customer subscription and send a payment link.
  • Access is enabled manually, after someone confirms payment over chat.
  • There is no record of who created a customer, what they were charged, or when payment arrived.
  • Renewals, invoicing, and payment retries are followed up by hand.

The product works. The commercial layer around it doesn't exist yet. V0 establishes the minimum viable foundation: create a customer-specific plan in Zoho Billing, generate a signup link, let the customer onboard and pay through Zoho's hosted checkout, and enforce access based on subscription status.

Customer Lifecycle

The complete journey a customer goes through, from plan creation to cancellation. Subscription status is managed by Zoho Billing. AIA reads subscription state from Zoho's API and webhooks to control product access. Status advances only when CS takes an explicit action or a Zoho billing event occurs.

High-Level Approach & Goals

This is not a self-serve billing system. AIA is sold through demos and that stays the same.

What we're building in V0 is a minimal control layer, the thinnest possible thing that takes a closed deal to a paying customer without manual coordination. No trials. No grace periods. No module-level access control. Those come in a later version. V0 is about getting the foundation right: one place to provision a plan, one link to send, one hosted checkout for payment, and access that turns on and off based on billing status.

The split is simple:

  • Zoho Billing handles subscription management, payment collection, invoicing, tax computation (GST), and payment retries
  • AIA handles the customer lifecycle: plan selection, signup link generation, onboarding, product provisioning, and access control

What this is not (V0)

No trial periods. No grace period — a missed payment moves the account to past_due and access is restricted immediately. No per-module access control, all modules are always on. No standalone customer portal build-out; Zoho's Customer Portal is used as-is. No self-serve signup. No public pricing page. No automated pricing recommendation; CS selects the plan. No token tracking. No usage-based billing. Those come in V1 and beyond.

Solution & Key Features

One module. Two surfaces: an admin portal for the CS and Growth team, and a billing touchpoint inside the AIA product for the customer. The customer-facing surface in V0 is minimal. It is a single "Billing & Payments" button inside the AIA product that redirects to Zoho's Customer Portal (invite-based) for subscription and invoice management. That button is only reachable after the customer has paid and their subscription is active.

CS owns the full activation lifecycle, from provisioning the plan to sharing the signup link. Zoho Billing handles everything after the customer is redirected to the hosted payment page. The AIA product handles onboarding and access control.

What Happens When

What happens when a customer pays?

Zoho processes the payment on the hosted page. The customer is redirected back to AIA's redirect_url (the /create-organisation page) with the hostedpage_id in query parameters. AIA calls GET /hostedpages/{hostedpage_id} to confirm payment success (checks status = success and data.subscription.status = live) and activates the account. The organisation/workspace is created in AIA, product access is enabled, and an audit entry is written. Zoho also fires a webhook separately as a backup confirmation.

How does the hosted payment page get the customer's details?

When AIA calls POST /hostedpages/newsubscription, it passes the customer object (with name and email collected from the AIA sign-up form) and the plan object (with the plan code) in a single request. Zoho internally creates the customer, subscription, and hosted page, and pre-fills the checkout page with the customer's details automatically. This happens at signup time, not at link-generation time, so the customer's actual name and email are used.

What happens when a payment fails?

Zoho sets the subscription to past_due and fires a webhook. AIA sets the account status to past_due and restricts product access. Zoho continues retrying according to its internal retry schedule. If all retries are exhausted, Zoho moves the subscription to dunning and fires another webhook. CS sees the account flagged in the Overview attention strip and follows up with the customer directly. When the customer pays, Zoho fires live status and access is restored.

What happens when a subscription is cancelled?

Zoho fires a cancelled webhook. AIA sets the account to cancelled. Product access is disabled immediately. If the customer wants to return, CS generates a new signup link and the cycle starts again.

The customer just paid but the admin portal still shows Payment Pending. What now?

CS opens Customer Detail, Subscription tab, and clicks Resync from Zoho. This triggers a live GET /subscriptions/{id} API call. If payment has landed, the status updates to active immediately. No customer action is needed.

Can a cancelled/expired customer come back?

Yes. CS opens the customer record, clicks Regenerate Signup Link, and shares the new URL. The existing Zoho customer is reused; a new subscription and hosted page are created. When the customer pays, the account moves to active again.

How does AIA find the right customer when a webhook arrives?

The Zoho webhook payload includes the subscription_id. AIA looks up the billing record by zohoSubscriptionId. If no match is found, the event falls back to matching by customer_id from the payload against zohoCustomerId. If neither matches, the event is logged but no state change is made.

What happens when the same webhook arrives twice?

The webhook handler checks the event's unique ID (from the Zoho webhook payload) against the last 1,000 processed IDs stored in the audit log. If the ID is found, the event is acknowledged (200 OK) but not processed again. This prevents double-status-changes from retry storms.

What happens when Zoho Billing is unreachable?

The Resync from Zoho call fails gracefully with an error message. The Overview page depends on AIA's local status (last synced state). Webhook deliveries that fail are retried by Zoho. If AIA remains unreachable, Zoho queues webhooks and retries. No account is incorrectly activated without a successful Zoho API confirmation.

How does Growth know the first payment landed?

The Zoho hosted page callback (via redirect_url) provides near-immediate confirmation. AIA fetches the hosted page data, verifies payment, and updates the account. The customer moves from Onboarding to Active in the customer list and disappears from the attention strip. The webhook serves as a backup confirmation. The audit log records both the callback and webhook events.

What exactly is logged in the audit trail?

Every change to a billing record is recorded. Each entry shows who made the change (actor), what changed (old value and new value), why (reason), and when (timestamp). Zoho webhook events are also logged with the event type as the action. Hosted page callbacks are logged as payment_confirmed events.

Do Growth and CS have different permissions?

Both have full access to the admin portal. The difference is who does what, not what they can see or do. Any @karboncard.com account can sign in and access all features.

What is the OTP login flow in production vs development?

In development, the OTP is returned in the redirect URL and displayed as a yellow badge on the verify page. In production, an email service (e.g. Resend) must be wired into the /api/auth/send-otp route to deliver the code. The session TTL is 8 hours. OTPs expire in 10 minutes.

How does the customer access the Zoho billing portal from inside AIA?

When the subscription is created, AIA invites the customer to Zoho's Customer Portal (triggered via Zoho settings — auto-enable for customers who complete hosted page payments). The customer receives an invite email from Zoho with their portal URL, username, and password. Inside the AIA product, a "Billing & Payments" button links to the Zoho Customer Portal URL. The customer logs in with their Zoho credentials and can view invoices, update payment method, and manage their subscription.

What happens when the customer clicks "Billing & Payments" and their account is not yet active?

The "Billing & Payments" button is controlled by the AIA product and is only shown when the customer is active. If called before the hosted page flow is complete, the Zoho Customer Portal invite may not yet have been sent. In practice this path cannot be reached because the button is not shown until after first payment.

What happens when a customer is deleted?

The billing record is permanently removed from the database. This cannot be undone. The confirmation modal requires typing the exact company name. The Zoho subscription is cancelled via API (POST /subscriptions/{id}/cancel). Any future Zoho webhooks for that customer will not find a matching record and will be silently ignored.

What is a Plan and how is it different from the legacy plan preset concept?

In V0 (Zoho), a plan is a real Zoho Billing plan created via the API. It has a plan_code, name, recurring price, billing interval, product association, and optional HSN/SAC code for GST. Plans are the source of truth for pricing. When CS generates a signup link, the plan is referenced by its plan_code in the subscription creation call. Unlike the earlier Dodo-based design (where "plan presets" were local templates and Dodo products were created per-customer at checkout time), Zoho plans are created once and reused across customers. Price can optionally be overridden per subscription.

What happens with Zoho rate limits?

Zoho Billing API allows 100 requests per minute per organisation with daily caps (1,000/day on Standard, 5,000/day on Premium). AIA's admin operations (customer creation, plan listing, subscription lookup) and webhook processing are designed to stay well within these limits. The Resync from Zoho operation is triggered manually by CS, not called automatically on page load. Webhook processing is event-driven and low-volume.

What happens when a hosted page expires?

Zoho hosted pages expire 1 hour after creation. Since the hosted page is created at signup time (when the customer is actively in the flow), 1 hour is normally sufficient. If a customer starts the signup flow, abandons, and returns after the hosted page has expired, they can contact CS to get a fresh signup link. The old Zoho subscription (created during the hosted page call) may remain in an unpaid/unconfirmed state. CS can regenerate the signup link, and when the customer signs up again, a fresh POST /hostedpages/newsubscription call creates everything anew.

Why does AIA create the subscription and hosted page together at signup time?

Zoho's POST /hostedpages/newsubscription API creates the customer, subscription, and hosted payment page in a single call. This is the cleanest orchestration point: the customer is mid-signup, we have their name and email, and Zoho handles all the internal resource creation. The subscription is created in live status (with auto-charge enabled) but the first payment is collected through the hosted page. AIA does not activate the organisation or enable product access until payment is confirmed via the hosted page callback or webhook. This is by design: the subscription exists in Zoho, but product access in AIA is gated on confirmed payment.

How does AIA handle the gap between hosted page callback and webhook?

The hosted page callback (redirect from Zoho after payment) is the first confirmation. AIA fetches GET /hostedpages/{id} and checks data.subscription.status to confirm payment. If the callback is missed (customer closes browser, network issue), the Zoho webhook (which fires asynchronously) serves as backup. AIA processes both paths idempotently — whichever arrives first sets the status, and the second is ignored as a no-op.
