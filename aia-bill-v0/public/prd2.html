AI Accountant Billing & Access PRD

AI Accountant · Billing and Access Control Module

Status: Draft Updated: 19 May 2026

Problem Statement

AIA automates financial document processing for Indian SMBs: Accounts Payable, Accounts Receivable, Bank Statements, GST Reconciliation, Reporting, and Tally / Zoho integration.

Every customer today goes through a sales conversation before they can use the product. That's intentional. What's not intentional is everything that happens after the deal closes.

There is no billing module.

  • Customers don't know what plan they're on.
  • Customers don't know what they've paid for or when it renews.
  • The team has no single place to activate a customer, assign modules, or track payment.
  • Access is enabled manually, after someone confirms payment over chat.
  • Renewals are followed up by hand.

The product works. The commercial layer around it doesn't exist yet.

Customer Lifecycle

The complete journey a customer goes through, from demo to offboarding. Status is set automatically by Dodo Payments via webhooks. The only manual status intervention available to CS is Freeze and Unfreeze.

High-Level Approach & Goals

This is not a self-serve billing system. AIA is sold through demos and that stays the same.

What we're building is a control layer, a thin module that connects a closed deal to an active, paying customer without manual coordination.

The split is simple:

  • Dodo Payments handles money: checkout, payments, subscriptions, invoices, renewals
  • AIA handles access: which customer is active, which modules they can use

What this is not

No self-serve signup. No public pricing page. No free trial without Growth approval. No automated pricing recommendation — Growth picks the plan. No token tracking. No usage-based billing. Those come later.

Solution & Key Features

One module. Two surfaces: an admin side for Growth and CS, and a customer side for the end user.

Growth owns the demo and first payment. Once payment lands, the account hands off to CS for ongoing management: renewals, exceptions, module changes, and everything else.

  • Opens Overview
  • Sees stat cards: Active, Trial, Grace, Frozen, Payment Pending

  • Opens Customer Detail → Overview tab

  • Dodo customer created (if new); checkout session created; URL copied to clipboard
  • Pastes or emails the link to the customer

  • Opens Packages
  • Adds, edits, activates, deactivates, or deletes plans

  • Opens Audit Log
  • Sees every status, module, plan, and note change with actor, old value, new value, reason, and timestamp
  • Dodo webhook events are also logged

End-user (AI Accountant Customer)

  • Opens their customer portal
  • Sees current plan, price, billing frequency, renewal date
  • Sees included modules and locked modules
  • Opens Dodo billing portal via Manage Billing for invoices and payment methods

As a customer, I want my portal to show the right status the moment I pay — without waiting for CS.

  • Pays on Dodo checkout; redirected to https://app.aiaccountant.com/
  • Opens portal — auto-resync from Dodo fires on load; status updates to active immediately
  • If resync hasn't triggered yet: clicks "I've Paid — Check Status" to force it

As a customer, I want to ask for access to a module I do not have without emailing.

  • Clicks Request Access on a locked module
  • Adds a short message; submits
  • CS reviews and approves or rejects with a reason

As a customer, I want clear instructions when something is wrong with my account.

  • If payment pending: full-page gate with Complete Payment button and "I've Paid — Check Status"
  • If trial expired: dashboard visible, modules locked, directed to contact the growth team
  • If grace: warning banner with grace end date; access retained
  • If frozen or inactive: full-page gate with Manage Billing and Contact CS

Automated — Dodo Payments

Dodo Payments notifies AIA whenever a subscription or payment event occurs, keeping status in sync without manual work.

  • Every incoming webhook is verified via Standard Webhooks signature
  • Every event is deduplicated — never processed twice
  • Payment succeeds → status: active; renewal date set; Dodo sends confirmation email to customer
  • Renewal paid → renewal date bumped forward
  • Payment fails → status: grace; 10-day window to pay
  • Payment received during grace → status: active; grace cleared
  • Grace expires without payment → status: frozen
  • Subscription cancelled → status: inactive
  • Plan changed in Dodo → status: active (re-activation)

What Happens When

What happens when a customer pays?

Dodo fires a subscription.active webhook. AIA sets the account to active, records activatedAt, and sets the renewal date from Dodo's next_billing_date. Dodo also sends a payment confirmation email to the customer automatically. The customer is redirected to https://app.aiaccountant.com/ after checkout completes.

The customer just paid but the portal still shows Payment Required. What now?

The portal auto-resyncs from Dodo on every load when status is payment_pending and a Dodo customer ID is linked. If the webhook has already fired, the portal unlocks on the next page load. If not, the customer can click "I've Paid — Check Status" to force a live pull from Dodo. No CS action needed.

What happens when a customer cancels during trial?

Dodo fires a subscription.cancelled webhook. The account moves to inactive. The customer can no longer access the product.

Can a frozen or inactive customer come back?

Yes. If frozen by Dodo (grace expired): the customer pays, Dodo fires payment.succeeded, account moves to active automatically. If frozen manually by CS: CS unfreezes the account with a reason — status moves to active. If inactive: Growth generates a new checkout link; on payment, account activates again.

What happens when a plan is changed mid-cycle?

Plan changes on active subscriptions are not handled in this version — changing the plan locally after checkout would diverge from what Dodo is billing. CS can update module access directly (Update Modules in Admin Actions) without touching the plan. A full plan change requires a new Dodo subscription.

What happens if the plan has no Dodo product ID?

Checkout generation fails with a clear error: "Sync the plan to Dodo first." CS goes to Packages, clicks the "Sync to Dodo" button on the affected plan, then retries checkout. This only happens if the auto-sync failed when the plan was first saved.

How does Growth know the first payment landed?

Dodo's webhook updates the account to active automatically. The customer moves from payment pending to active in the customer list. Growth can see this without any manual check. The audit log also records the webhook event.

What happens when a phone number is invalid during checkout?

The system strips spaces and non-E.164 characters before sending to Dodo. If the result is not a valid E.164 number (must start with + followed by 10–15 digits), the phone field is silently omitted. Dodo does not require phone. Checkout proceeds.

What happens when billing.json is empty (fresh deploy)?

The admin dashboard POSTs localStorage data to /api/sync on first mount. The server writes to billing.json only if it is empty. This seeds the server store with whatever the browser has — including demo data — so webhook handlers and the customer portal can find customers from that point on.

What exactly is logged in the audit trail?

Every change to a customer, plan, or module assignment is recorded. Each entry shows who made the change (actor), what changed (old value and new value), why (the reason typed in), and when (timestamp). Dodo webhook events are also logged with the event type as the action.

Do Growth and CS have different permissions?

Both have full access to the admin portal. The difference is who does what — not what they can see or do. Growth handles demos and first activation. CS handles everything after. Any @karboncard.com account can sign in and access all features.

What is the OTP login flow in production vs development?

In development, the OTP is returned in the redirect URL and displayed as a yellow badge on the verify page. In production, an email service (e.g. Resend) must be wired into the /api/auth/send-otp route to deliver the code. The session TTL is 8 hours. OTPs expire in 10 minutes.

How does the customer access the Dodo billing portal?

From their customer portal, the customer clicks Manage Billing. This calls /api/dodo/customer-portal, which creates a short-lived Dodo portal session and redirects the customer to it. The customer does not need a separate Dodo login — the session is generated server-side using their Dodo customer ID.

What if a webhook fires for a customer not in billing.json?

The webhook handler tries to match by metadata.customer_account_id (set at checkout creation), then falls back to matching by dodoCustomerId. If neither matches, the event is logged but no state change is made. This only happens if the customer snapshot was not written to billing.json during checkout — which is covered by the customerSnapshot field sent at checkout creation.

What happens when the same webhook arrives twice?

The webhook handler checks the webhook-id header against the last 1,000 processed IDs stored in .data/webhook-events.json. If the ID is found, the event is acknowledged (200 OK) but not processed again. This prevents double-status-changes from retry storms.

What happens when a customer is deleted?

The customer record is permanently removed from localStorage and billing.json. This cannot be undone. The confirmation modal requires typing the exact company name. Any future Dodo webhooks for that customer will not find a matching record and will be silently ignored.
